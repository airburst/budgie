Think of envelope budgeting like a digital version of an old-school cash system. Back in the day, people would literally put cash into physical envelopes labeled "Rent," "Groceries," and "Fun." When an envelope was empty, that was it—you stopped spending in that category.

Banktivity applies this logic to your modern digital accounts. Here is the breakdown of how it works:

---

## 1. The Core Philosophy: Give Every Pound a Job

Instead of looking at your total bank balance and thinking, "I have £2,000," you break that total down. You ask: **"What does this money need to do before I get paid again?"**

- **Priority One:** Fixed bills (Mortgage, Utilities).
- **Priority Two:** Variable necessities (Groceries, Petrol).
- **Priority Three:** Long-term goals (Holiday fund, New car).
- **Priority Four:** Lifestyle (Dining out, Subscriptions).

## 2. The Mechanics: How it Works in the App

Banktivity uses a specific workflow to keep you on track:

| Feature          | Action                                                                                                        |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **The "Bucket"** | You create digital envelopes (categories) for all your expenses.                                              |
| **Funding**      | When income hits your account, you "fill" your envelopes until your **Available to Budget** balance is zero.  |
| **The Spend**    | As you buy things, Banktivity automatically deducts that amount from the specific envelope.                   |
| **The Pivot**    | If you overspend on "Dining Out," you must "steal" money from another envelope (like "Clothing") to cover it. |

## 3. Why It’s "Zero-Based"

The term **Zero-Based** doesn't mean you have £0 in your bank account; it means your **Total Income minus your Budgeted Expenses equals zero**.

If you have £500 left over after covering your bills, you don't leave it sitting there "unassigned." You give it a job—even if that job is just sitting in a "Rainy Day" envelope. This prevents "accidental spending" where money just seems to disappear.

---

### Why this is better than "Traditional" Budgeting

Most people budget by looking backward—seeing what they _already_ spent. Banktivity's system is **forward-looking**. It forces you to make decisions _before_ you tap your card.

> **The Golden Rule:** You are only budgeting the money you **actually have** right now, not the money you expect to earn three weeks from now.
