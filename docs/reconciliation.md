Reconciling your bank account is essentially a "sanity check" to ensure your records match the bank's records down to the penny. In software like Quicken, the process is streamlined but still requires a bit of focused attention.

Here is the step-by-step workflow to get your books in balance.

1. Preparation
   Before you click the reconcile button, ensure you have your bank statement (either paper or a PDF) for the period you are closing.

Check for "uncleared" transactions: Make sure you’ve entered all your recent checks, ATM withdrawals, and deposits into the Quicken register.

Update your accounts: If you use "One Step Update," run it now to pull in the latest cleared transactions from your bank.

2. Initiation
   Open the account register you wish to reconcile and look for the Reconcile button (usually found in the top toolbar or under the "Account Actions" gear icon).

A dialog box will appear asking for two key pieces of information from your statement:

Ending Balance: Enter the final balance shown on your statement.

Statement Ending Date: Enter the date the statement period closed.

3. The Matching Process
   Quicken will present a list of transactions that have not yet been "reconciled." Your goal is to check off items in the software that appear on your statement.

Deposits and Credits: Mark every deposit that appears on your statement.

Checks and Debits: Mark every check, debit card purchase, or bill pay that appears on your statement.

The "Difference" Counter: Look for a box (usually in the bottom right) labeled Difference. Your goal is to get this number to 0.00.

4. Handling Discrepancies
   If your difference isn't zero, don't panic. It’s usually caused by one of three things:

Service Fees/Interest: You might have missed a small bank fee or a few cents of interest. You can add these directly in the reconciliation window.

Missing Transactions: You forgot to record a stray coffee run or an automated payment. Add it to the register now.

Transposed Numbers: You recorded a check as $54.00, but the bank processed it as $45.00. Correct the entry in your register to match the bank.

5. Finalizing
   Once the "Difference" hits zero, click Finished.

Quicken will update the status of these transactions from "Cleared" (indicated by a 'c') to "Reconciled" (indicated by an 'R'). This "R" status locks the transaction to prevent accidental changes in the future.
